/// https://www.reddit.com/r/EnglishLearning/comments/1lrghqv/why_do_some_people_say_a_proof_or_an_evidence/
/// The full construction here is "...gave me a different kind of evidence. An evidence that..."
/// The speaker is referring not to a specific piece of evidence but a collection, type or kind of evidence.
/// It's common to see this kind of structure in dialogue for dramatic effect. Consider the following:
/// "I had a new feeling. A feeling that made me want to gag."
/// "I tasted a new water. A water that made me strong."
/// "I had a new gold. A gold that shone black as tar."
