ides


Alternative forms: Id., Ides

noun &nbsp; (plural ides)


1. {{lb|en|historical|often|_|capitalized}} The notional [[full moon|full-moon]] day of a Roman month, occurring on the 15th day of the four original 31-day months (March, May, Quintilis or July, and October) and on the 13th day of all other months.

Coordinate terms: calends, nones

Etymology: See [[ide]].
